Museu Del Ocultismo

  • The Occult Museum, also known as The Warren's Occult Museum, was a private collection maintained by renowned demonologists Ed and Lorraine Warren. Located in Monroe, Connecticut, United States, the museum housed a variety of objects and artifacts allegedly connected to paranormal activities and supernatural events.

    The museum's collection included items such as possessed dolls, artifacts used in witchcraft rituals, demonic relics, protective amulets, photographs, and evidence from cases investigated by the Warrens over the course of decades. Each object had its own story and claims of paranormal phenomena associated with it.

    The museum was established with the purpose of providing a safe place to store and study these supernatural objects, while also serving as an educational space for visitors to learn about the world of the occult and unexplained phenomena. Ed and Lorraine Warren were committed to sharing their knowledge and experiences with the public, seeking to increase understanding and awareness about the paranormal world.

Snedeker family house

  • The Snedeker family house is known for being a haunted location, with reports of paranormal activity and supernatural events. Located in Southington, Connecticut, United States, the house was the setting for a series of disturbing occurrences experienced by the Snedeker family.

    According to reports, the Snedeker family moved into the house in the 1980s and soon began to experience unexplained phenomena, including apparitions, strange voices, unpleasant odors, and even physical assaults by invisible entities. These terrifying events deeply affected the family, causing them great distress and suffering.

    The story of the Snedeker family house gained notoriety after being publicized by Ed and Lorraine Warren, renowned paranormal investigators. They conducted an investigation at the location and concluded that the house was infested with malevolent entities.

    The Snedeker family's experiences and the Warrens' investigation inspired the production of a film titled "The Haunting in Connecticut," released in 2009, which depicts the supernatural events that took place in the house.

    The Snedeker family house continues to be of interest to paranormal enthusiasts and is considered a notorious haunted location in the United States.

Perron family house

  • The Perron Family House was the setting for a series of disturbing events that haunted the lives of its inhabitants. The Perron family, consisting of parents Roger and Carolyn and their five daughters, moved into the house in Harrisville, Rhode Island, seeking a peaceful life in the countryside. However, they soon found themselves confronted by a supernatural and threatening presence.

    Over time, the family witnessed a series of inexplicable phenomena. They would hear whispering voices, knocks on the walls during the night, and doors opening and closing without apparent reason. Objects were moved and thrown with force, frightening and intimidating everyone. In addition, family members reported seeing shadowy apparitions and a constant feeling of being watched.

    Faced with these terrifying events, the Perron family decided to seek the help of paranormal investigators Ed and Lorraine Warren. The Warrens conducted several investigations in the house, using specialized techniques and tools to document and understand the nature of the paranormal activities.

    The case of the Perron Family House gained media attention and captured the interest of the public. It was widely publicized and became one of the most well-known and iconic cases associated with the Warrens' work. The story of the Perron family and their struggle against supernatural forces was portrayed in the 2013 film "The Conjuring," further increasing the case's fame.

    The Perron Family House remains a poignant example of an intense and unsettling haunting. The legacy of this case continues to intrigue and fascinate those interested in the world of the paranormal and the mysteries surrounding the interactions between the spiritual realm and our own.

Witch Bathsheba Sherman

  • Bathsheba Sherman was a real woman who lived in the 19th century in Rhode Island, United States. She was a farmer and the wife of a man named Judson Sherman. Bathsheba's reputation became tarnished due to allegations of witchcraft and dark practices.

    There are historical records indicating that Bathsheba had four children, but they all died at a young age. These tragedies were attributed to alleged supernatural activities associated with Bathsheba. She was accused of sacrificing babies in Satanic rituals and making pacts with evil forces.

    However, there is no concrete evidence to support the accusations against Bathsheba. Many of the stories and legends surrounding her life have been amplified over the years, taking on darker and more sinister tones.

    The connection between Bathsheba Sherman and the Perron family house in Harrisville, where purportedly supernatural events occurred, is questionable. Some sources claim that the Perron family brought Bathsheba's story to light during the paranormal investigation, while others suggest that there is no direct link between her and the house.

    It is important to note that Bathsheba Sherman's story was popularized and romanticized in the films of "The Conjuring" franchise. While her true story remains shrouded in mystery, she has become a well-known figure in folklore and horror narratives related to the cases investigated by the Warren couple.

Smurl family house

  • The Smurl family house refers to the residence of the Smurl family located in West Pittston, Pennsylvania, United States. The Smurl family, consisting of Jack and Janet Smurl and their children, reported experiencing a series of paranormal phenomena in their home during the 1980s.

    Disturbances in the Smurl family house included unexplained sounds, objects moving on their own, unpleasant odors, and physical attacks. The family claimed to have witnessed apparitions, heard voices, and encountered violent encounters with invisible entities. These alleged events gained significant media attention and sparked the interest of paranormal investigators, including Ed and Lorraine Warren.

    The case of the Smurl family became widely known as "The Haunting of the Smurl Family" and was the subject of extensive investigation and scrutiny. The events in their home generated both skepticism and belief, with debates surrounding the authenticity of the reported phenomena.

    While some skeptics dismissed the claims as hoaxes or misinterpretations, the Smurl family maintained that they lived in a haunted house and endured years of terrifying experiences. The case remains controversial and is often cited as one of the notable paranormal cases investigated by the Warrens.

    The story of the Smurl family has been documented in books and featured in television programs, further increasing its notoriety in the field of paranormal research and haunted house narratives
