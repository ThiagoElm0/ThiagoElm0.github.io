About

Casal Warren

Edward "Ed" Warren Miney (7 de setembro de 1926 — 23 de agosto de 2006) e Lorraine Rita Warren (31 de janeiro de 1927 — 18 de abril de 2019) foram investigadores de fenômenos paranormais e autores associados com casos de destaque de assombração. Edward foi um veterano da Marinha na Segunda Guerra Mundial e ex-oficial de polícia que tornou-se um autodidata, especialista em demonologia, autor e conferencista. Sua esposa Lorraine era uma clarividente e médium de transe leve que trabalhou com o marido.

Em 1952, os Warren fundaram a New England Society for Psychic Research (N.E.S.P.R.), o mais antigo grupo de caça-fantasmas na Nova Inglaterra. Eles escreveram vários livros sobre o paranormal e sobre suas investigações privadas em vários relatos de atividade paranormal. Eles alegaram ter investigado mais de 10.000 casos durante sua carreira. Os Warren estavam entre os primeiros investigadores no polêmico The Amityville Horror. De acordo com o Warren, o N.E.S.P.R. utiliza uma variedade de indivíduos, incluindo médicos, pesquisadores, policiais, enfermeiros, estudantes universitários e membros do clero em suas investigações.

Os Warren foram responsáveis pelo treinamento de vários demonólogos, incluindo Dave Considine e seu sobrinho John Zaffis. Além das investigações, Lorraine dirigiu o Museu Oculto dos Warren no porão de sua casa em Monroe, Connecticut, com a ajuda de seu genro, Tony Spera.

Warren's main cases

  • Annabelle

    The real case of Annabelle involves a possessed doll that gained international attention for its alleged paranormal activities. The story began when a nursing student named Donna received the doll as a gift from her mother in the early 1970s. Donna and her roommate Angie started noticing strange occurrences in their apartment shortly after bringing Annabelle into their home.

    The doll seemed to have a life of its own, moving around the apartment, changing positions, and even leaving handwritten notes. It also appeared to be possessed, as it would often change facial expressions and emit eerie sounds. The girls soon realized that there was something deeply unsettling about the doll and sought help.

    They contacted paranormal investigators Ed and Lorraine Warren, who determined that Annabelle was not an ordinary doll but instead a conduit for a malevolent entity. According to the Warrens, the doll was inhabited by the spirit of a deceased girl named Annabelle Higgins. They believed that the doll was manipulating its surroundings and causing harm to those who came into contact with it.

    The Warrens eventually took possession of the doll, deeming it too dangerous to be kept with the public. They placed Annabelle in a secure case in their Occult Museum, where it remains to this day. The case and the doll have become popular subjects in the realm of paranormal investigations, inspiring movies such as "The Conjuring" series and its spin-off film "Annabelle".

    While skeptics question the authenticity of the case, the story of Annabelle continues to fascinate and terrify people around the world.

  • The Perron Family

    The Perron family is known for having lived a haunting experience in their home in the 1970s. Roger and Carolyn Perron moved with their five daughters to an old farmhouse in Harrisville, Rhode Island. Shortly after the move, they began to face disturbing paranormal phenomena in the house.

    The Perrons reported witnessing supernatural activities such as doors opening and closing on their own, furniture moving, whispering voices, apparitions of spirits, and even physical assaults by unseen entities. The family endured years of terror and suffering before seeking help.

    Renowned paranormal investigators Ed and Lorraine Warren were called to investigate the Perron family's house. They confirmed the presence of malevolent forces and assisted the family in coping with and resolving the supernatural events.

    The case of the Perron family became widely known and was one of the most documented and studied cases in the field of paranormal activity. Their story inspired the movie "The Conjuring" (2013), directed by James Wan, which portrays the terrifying events experienced by the family.

    While some question the veracity of the case, the accounts of the Perron family continue to intrigue and chill those interested in paranormal activities and hauntings.

  • the smurl family

    The Smurl family, consisting of Jack and Janet Smurl and their two children, was another family that faced a frightening and supernatural experience. The story of the Smurl family involved a case of poltergeist activity that occurred in their home located in West Pittston, Pennsylvania.

    The strange and disturbing events began to unfold in the 1980s. The family reported a series of paranormal phenomena, including unexplained noises, objects moving on their own, furniture being shaken, and even appearances of terrifying entities.

    The Smurls encountered intense poltergeist activity that affected their daily lives and caused significant emotional distress. They sought the help of experts and paranormal investigators, including the renowned demonologists Ed and Lorraine Warren.

    During the time the Smurl family lived in that house, they experienced moments of great anguish and fear. They reported that the supernatural presence was aggressive and unsettling, causing a significant impact on their health and well-being.

    The case of the Smurl family attracted media attention and public interest, becoming a notable example of a poltergeist haunting. The story of the Smurl family is widely known as one of the most frightening and disturbing cases of paranormal activity ever recorded in the United States.

    Despite the challenges they faced, the Smurl family remained united and found strength to cope with the supernatural circumstances. Their story serves as a reminder of the unknown and mysterious realities that may exist beyond our rational understanding.

  • Trial of Arne Cheyenne Johnson

    The trial of Arne Cheyenne Johnson took place in 1981 in the United States. Arne Cheyenne Johnson was accused of murdering his landlord, Alan Bono, during an altercation. The case became known as "The Devil Made Me Do It" case due to the defense's claim that Johnson was possessed by a demon at the time of the crime.

    Johnson's defense argued that he was under the influence of supernatural forces and that demonic possession was the cause of the murder. They cited the prior involvement of the couple Ed and Lorraine Warren, renowned paranormal investigators, in cases related to Johnson's family as evidence.

    However, the judge ruled that the claim of demonic possession was not a legally valid defense for the charge of murder. The trial proceeded based on conventional evidence, and Johnson was found guilty of manslaughter and received a reduced sentence.

    The trial of Arne Cheyenne Johnson is notable because it marked the first time in U.S. legal history that a defense of demonic possession was presented in court. It garnered significant media attention and inspired movies and books on the subject.

  • Amityville Horror

    Amityville Horror is a famous case of paranormal activity that took place in a house located in Amityville, New York, United States. In 1974, the Lutz family, composed of George and Kathy Lutz and their three children, moved into the house where a gruesome murder had occurred a year earlier. Ronald DeFeo Jr. brutally killed his family while they were sleeping in that house.

    Shortly after the move, the Lutz family began experiencing a series of terrifying and unexplained events. They reported phenomena such as whispering voices, putrid odors, doors and windows opening and closing on their own, and witnessing chilling apparitions. There were also reports of an evil presence haunting the house.

    The Lutz family contacted the paranormal investigators Ed and Lorraine Warren to help deal with the paranormal activity. The Warrens conducted several investigations and studies in the house, documenting inexplicable phenomena and capturing audio and video evidence.

    The Amityville case gained widespread media attention and sparked public interest worldwide. Numerous works have been produced on the subject, including books, films, and documentaries. The shocking and terrifying story of the Amityville house has become one of the most well-known and studied cases in the field of paranormal activity.

    Despite the controversy and debate surrounding the veracity of the events, the Amityville case remains one of the most iconic and discussed cases in the realm of the paranormal, influencing popular culture and inspiring many other horror narratives over the years.
